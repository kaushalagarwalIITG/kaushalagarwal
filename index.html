About Me

I am a Ph.D. Scholar at the School of Agro and Rural Technology, Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati. My research focuses on phytofluidics, an emerging interdisciplinary field that combines plant biology, microfluidics, transport phenomena, biomechanics, and advanced imaging to investigate how plants respond to their surrounding environment.

Research Vision

My goal is to develop microfluidic platforms that recreate realistic plant microenvironments, enabling non-invasive studies of root growth, nutrient transport, mechanical interactions, and stress adaptation. These technologies aim to advance fundamental plant science while supporting sustainable agriculture and precision farming.

Research Highlights

🌱 Phytofluidics

Developing Plant-on-a-Chip platforms to study root growth, nutrient transport, and plant responses under controlled microenvironments.

🪴 Soil Matrix-on-a-Chip (SMaC)

A transparent platform that recreates structured soil environments to investigate root morphodynamics and root–soil interactions.

🧪 Plant Stress Biology

Understanding how nutrient flow, salinity, and mechanical stimuli regulate plant growth, metabolism, and stress tolerance.

🔬 Microfluidics & Imaging

Integrating microfluidics with microscopy, Raman spectroscopy, and computational analysis for real-time plant phenotyping.
