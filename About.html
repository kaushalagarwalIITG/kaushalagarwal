About Me

I am a Ph.D. Scholar at the School of Agro and Rural Technology, Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati. My research lies at the intersection of plant biology, microfluidics, biomechanics, and transport phenomena. I develop phytofluidic platforms that recreate realistic plant microenvironments, enabling non-invasive studies of root growth, nutrient transport, and plant responses to environmental stress. My long-term goal is to advance phytofluidics as an interdisciplinary research field and contribute to sustainable agriculture through innovative experimental technologies.

Current Research Focus

My current work focuses on developing phytofluidic platforms to investigate root growth, nutrient transport, plant biomechanics, and stress responses under controlled microenvironments. I am also interested in integrating advanced imaging, computational modelling, and biosensing for plant phenotyping and precision agriculture.


Research Philosophy

I believe that addressing complex biological questions requires an interdisciplinary approach that combines biology, engineering, and physics. By developing accessible experimental platforms and analytical tools, I aim to improve our understanding of plant–environment interactions and contribute to future technologies for sustainable agriculture and food security.
